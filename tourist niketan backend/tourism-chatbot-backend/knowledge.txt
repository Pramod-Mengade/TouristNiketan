Shaniwar Wada is a historical fortification in the city of Pune built in 1732. It was the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire. The fort is open to visitors from 8:00 AM to 6:30 PM. There is a light and sound show held in the evenings.

The Aga Khan Palace, built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III, is a historically significant landmark associated with India’s freedom struggle. Originally constructed to provide employment during a famine, it later became a detention center during the Quit India Movement, where Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, and Mahadev Desai were imprisoned. Kasturba Gandhi and Mahadev Desai both passed away here, and their memorials remain within the premises. Architecturally, the palace features an elegant Italianate style with large arches, spacious corridors, and expansive gardens. Today it functions as a museum displaying photographs, personal belongings, and artifacts related to Gandhi’s life, making it both a place of historical importance and a symbol of India’s struggle for independence.

Sinhagad Fort is a hill fortress located about 30 kilometers southwest of Pune. It is a very popular weekend trekking destination, especially during the monsoon season. You can reach the top by driving or by trekking up the mountain. Don't forget to try the local Pitla Bhakri and Kanda Bhaji served by vendors at the top.

The Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple is one of the most famous Hindu temples in Pune, dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It is located in the heart of the city. The temple is especially crowded and beautifully decorated during the 10-day Ganesh Chaturthi festival.

For traditional Maharashtrian breakfast, tourists must visit Vaishali restaurant on Fergusson College (FC) Road. It is famous for its South Indian food, especially the Mysore Masala Dosa, and is heavily frequented by local college students.

Pune is famous for its bakeries. Kayani Bakery on East Street in the Camp area is a legendary establishment. Tourists should definitely buy their famous Shrewsbury biscuits and Mawa cakes. Be prepared to wait in line, as it gets very crowded.

If you want to try authentic Puneri Misal Pav, Katakirr Misal and Ramnath Misal are two highly recommended spots. Misal is a spicy curry made from sprouted moth beans, topped with farsan, and served with bread (pav). 

The Osho International Meditation Resort in Koregaon Park is a popular destination for international tourists seeking meditation and wellness retreats. Entry requires registration, an HIV test, and wearing specific maroon or white robes inside the campus.

Transportation in Pune relies heavily on auto-rickshaws and the PMPML city buses. When using auto-rickshaws, it is highly recommended to ask the driver to use the meter. Ride-hailing apps like Ola and Uber are also widely available and very reliable for getting around the city.

The Pune Metro is a rapid transit system currently expanding across the city. It is the most efficient way to travel between major hubs like Vanaz, Ramwadi, PCMC, and Swargate, avoiding the heavy city traffic.

The best time to visit Pune is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and cool. The monsoon season, from July to September, transforms the surrounding Sahyadri mountains into lush green landscapes, making it the perfect time for nature lovers and trekkers.

Pune is widely considered the cultural capital of Maharashtra. The primary local language spoken is Marathi. However, Hindi and English are widely understood and spoken by almost everyone in the city.

Kasba Ganapti is the Gram Daivat, or village deity, of Pune. Located near Lal Mahal, Kasba Peth, it holds the esteemed position of being the foremost honored Ganpati in Pune. As a result, it is given the privilege of leading the Ganesh Utsav Procession. The statue of Ganesh was originally installed by Jijabai, the mother of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Sarasbaug is a beautiful park adorned with lush green lawns and also offers a jogging track for fitness enthusiasts. One of the highlights of the park is a small hill surrounded by a serene lake, where you can find the renowned Ganesh temple known as ‘Talyatala Ganapati’.

The Parvati Hill is a serene and historically significant hilltop destination in Pune, rising about 640 meters above sea level and offering panoramic views of the city along with a peaceful spiritual atmosphere centered around the Parvati Temple, built during the Maratha Empire under Balaji Baji Rao. Visitors climb over 100 steps to reach the top, making it a popular spot for sunrise, sunset, and morning walks. Nearby areas like Erandwane and Deccan offer plenty of good food options such as Pimlico, Cafe Peter, Shreyas Siddhi, and local favorites like Katakirrr Misal for authentic Maharashtrian snacks. For stays, visitors can find comfortable accommodations nearby such as Ramee Grand Hotel & Spa, Hotel Shalimar, and budget-friendly options in the Parvati–Swargate area.

The Khadakwasla Dam, built on the Mutha River, is a crucial water reservoir that supplies water to Pune and its surrounding areas while also serving as a popular scenic spot for visitors. Located near the prestigious National Defence Academy and the Central Water and Power Research Station, the dam is surrounded by natural beauty and attracts people for evening outings and monsoon views. A short drive ahead leads to the historic Sinhagad Fort, while nearby reservoirs like Panshet Dam and Varasgaon Dam contribute mainly to irrigation and enhance the region’s appeal.

The Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum is a renowned cultural museum that showcases an extensive and diverse collection of artifacts reflecting the rich heritage of Maharashtra and India. Established by Dinkar G. Kelkar in memory of his son Raja, the museum houses over 20,000 objects including traditional household items, musical instruments, intricately carved doors and windows, sculptures, paintings, and decorative art. One of its most famous sections is the Mastani Mahal, which recreates the living quarters of Mastani, offering visitors a glimpse into Maratha-era lifestyle.

The Shivneri Fort is a historic hill fort dating back to the 17th century and holds immense importance as the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the legendary founder of the Maratha Empire. Located near Junnar, the fort was strategically built for defense with strong stone walls, guarded entrances, and a commanding view of the surrounding region. At its center lies the famous ‘Badami Talav’, a water reservoir that ensured a steady water supply, while the fort is further enriched by two perennial natural springs known as Ganga and Yamuna.

In case of emergencies in Pune or anywhere in Maharashtra, tourists can dial the toll-free number 112 for police, fire, or medical assistance.
